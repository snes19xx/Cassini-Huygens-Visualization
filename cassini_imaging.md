# TITAN

### **Textures by Spectrum and Filter**

| Texture Name             | Spectrum / Type                 | Cassini Instrument & Filter Details                                                                                                   | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **titan_IR**             | **False-Color Infrared (VIMS)** | **VIMS** (Visual and Infrared Mapping Spectrometer). Typical mapping: Red (5.0 $\mu m$), Green (2.0 $\mu m$), and Blue (1.3 $\mu m$). | Brown regions correspond largely to equatorial dune fields, while bluish areas are associated with stronger water-ice signatures or less organic surface cover                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **titan_visible**        | **Visible Light**               | **ISS** (Imaging Science Subsystem) using **RGB** (Red, Green, Blue) filters.                                                         | How Titan would appear to the human eye. The surface is completely obscured by a thick, orange-tinted photochemical haze.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **titan_false_color_IR** | **Near-Infrared (Surface)**     | **ISS** using the **CB3** filter centered at **938 nm**.                                                                              | Methane transparency windows in the near infrared allowed Cassini’s ISS camera to partially penetrate Titan’s atmospheric haze and map surface albedo variations. The dark equatorial regions, consist of organic hydrocarbon dunes formed by atmospheric fallout. Blue hues indicate exposed water ice or thinner organic coatings rather than liquid methane. |
| **titan_near_IR**        | **False-Color Near-IR**         | **ISS** Narrow-Angle Camera (NAC) utilizing a combination of infrared filters (e.g., **756 nm**, **889 nm**, and **938 nm**).         | This version uses infrared data but applies a "naturalistic" color palette to simulate surface details while maintaining the characteristic yellowish hue of Titan’s atmosphere.                                                                                                                                                                                |

# ENCELADUS

### **Textures by Spectrum and Filter**

| Texture Name     | Spectrum / Type                 | Cassini Instrument & Filter Details                                                                                           | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **enceladus_IR** | **False-Color Infrared (VIMS)** | **VIMS** (Visual and Infrared Mapping Spectrometer). Mapping: Red ($1.8 \mu m$), Green ($1.3 \mu m$), and Blue ($1.1 \mu m$). | High-contrast mapping of ice crystallinity. The intense red at the south pole (Enceladus' Tiger Stripes) highlights "fresh" crystalline water ice from active plumes, while teal/blue areas indicate older, radiation-weathered ice. |
| **enceladus**    | **False-Color Visible/Near-IR** | **ISS** (Imaging Science Subsystem) using **UV3** ($338 nm$), **Green** ($568 nm$), and **IR3** ($930 nm$) filters.           | Enhanced subtle color and albedo variations reveals the sharp transition between the ancient, cratered northern plains and the geologically young, fractured southern polar terrain.                                                 |
